Nature Based Solutions

Reforestation and Afforestation

Reforestation (the restoration of destroyed forests) and afforestation (the planting of trees in new places) increase biodiversity, reduce erosion, and function as carbon sinks, absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere to battle climate change.

Renewable Energy

Hydroelectric Power

Using the energy of flowing water, usually from rivers or reservoirs, hydroelectric power creates electricity by channeling the water through turbines that are connected to generators. This renewable energy source is reputable and well-established.delivering a steady stream of clean power with low emissions of greenhouse gases.

Solar Power

Solar power uses photovoltaic cells or solar panels to capture solar energy and turn it into electrical energy. It is a low-impact, clean, renewable energy source that may be used for both business and domestic purposes.

Wind Energy

Wind energy utilizes wind turbines to convert kinetic energy from the wind into electricity. Wind farms, located in areas with consistent wind patterns, generate clean and sustainable energy, contributing to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and dependence on fossil fuels..

Geothermal Energy

In order to provide electricity or heat and cool buildings, geothermal energy harnesses heat from the Earth's core. It entails drawing steam or hot water from subterranean reservoirs and using it to power heat exchangers or turbines . A dependable, sustainable energy source with little effect on the environment is geothermal energy.

Global Warming

Melting Ice Caps and Glaciers

Global warming accelerates the melting of polar ice caps, glaciers, and ice sheets, contributing to rising sea levels. This melting ice also disrupts ecosystems, affects wildlife habitats, and threatens communities living in coastal areas with increased risks of flooding and coastal erosion

Temperature Rise

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to the accumulation of greenhouse gases. This temperature rise leads to changes in weather patterns, increased frequency of extreme weather events like heatwaves and storms, and melting polar ice caps and glaciers.

Click anywhere to return

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O) trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to the greenhouse effect and global warming. These gases are released through human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

Impacts on Human Health

Global warming has direct and indirect effects on human health, including heat-related illnesses, respiratory problems due to air pollution, vector-borne diseases like malaria and dengue fever spreading to new regions, food insecurity, and mental health issues related to climate-related stress and displacement.
